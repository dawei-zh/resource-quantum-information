## Exercise 3.3.10

The Hadmard gate, Pauli $X$ gate and Pauli $Z$ gate are defined as
$$
H = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\begin{pmatrix}
1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1
\end{pmatrix}, \,X = \begin{pmatrix}
0 & 1\\ 1 &0
\end{pmatrix},\, Z = \begin{pmatrix}
1 & 0\\ 0 &-1
\end{pmatrix}\tag{1}
$$
Thus, we have
$$
\begin{align}
HXH &=\frac{1}{2}\begin{pmatrix}
1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1
\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}
0 & 1\\ 1 &0
\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}
1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1
\end{pmatrix} \\
&=  \frac{1}{2}\begin{pmatrix}
1 & 1\\ -1 & 1
\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}
1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1
\end{pmatrix}\\
&= \frac{1}{2}\begin{pmatrix}
2 & 0 \\ 0 & -2
\end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix}
1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1
\end{pmatrix} = Z
\end{align}\tag{2}
$$
Also,
$$
\begin{align}
HZH &=\frac{1}{2}\begin{pmatrix}
1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1
\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}
1 & 0\\ 0 &-1
\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}
1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1
\end{pmatrix} \\
&=  \frac{1}{2}\begin{pmatrix}
1 & -1\\ 1 & 1
\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}
1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1
\end{pmatrix}\\
&= \frac{1}{2}\begin{pmatrix}
0 & 2 \\ 2 & 0
\end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix}
0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0
\end{pmatrix} = X
\end{align}\tag{3}
$$
